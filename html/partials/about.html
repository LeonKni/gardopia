About Gardopia

How It Started

In 2012 our founder Stephen Lucke started a community garden at the University of the Incarnate Word.
The garden was born out of his love for wellness, including nutrition, exercise, and stewardship of the environment.
In conjunction with UIW, Stephen began collaborating with outside entities in impoverished communities to spread the seed of wellness.

Mission

The mission of Gardopia is to engage and educate communities in the natural environment while providing healthy food options to underprivileged communities.

Vision

Our vision is to have community and educational gardens throughout San Antonio, in turn reducing obesity, related diseases, and pollution.

Non-Profit

Gardopia builds and maintains many educational/community gardens at a reduced cost, providing a much needed service that is otherwise unaffordable in such communities.
